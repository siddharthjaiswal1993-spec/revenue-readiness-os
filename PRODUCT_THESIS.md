# Product Thesis — Revenue Readiness OS

---

## The Bet

Sales rep performance is partly a talent problem and mostly an information and feedback problem. Reps who struggle are often struggling because they lack practice, contextual knowledge, and timely coaching — not because they lack capability. Traditional enablement programmes address this with scheduled training events and static content libraries. These interventions have well-documented limits: information decays, events are infrequent, and coaching is inconsistent.

The bet: a continuous, AI-native readiness system that gives reps practice opportunities that simulate real buyers, gives managers actionable coaching queues grounded in skill data, and connects every readiness activity to pipeline and revenue outcomes will produce measurably better revenue performance than event-based enablement.

---

## The Problem

Sales organisations face five specific enablement failures:

1. **Ramp time is too long** — new reps take 6–12 months to reach full productivity, primarily because they lack structured practice in a low-stakes environment
2. **Coaching is inconsistent** — frontline managers coach based on intuition and recency bias, not skill data; the reps who get the most coaching are often not the reps who need it most
3. **Content is disconnected from outcomes** — training libraries exist but correlation between content consumption and deal outcomes is rarely measured
4. **Skill gaps are invisible until too late** — there is no continuous signal between a rep's skill level and their pipeline risk
5. **Enablement ROI is unprovable** — training completion rates are tracked; revenue impact is not

Each of these failures has the same root cause: enablement is designed as an event, not a system. Events are episodic, not continuous. They measure completion, not capability. They cannot connect training to outcomes.

---

## Why AI Changes This

Three specific AI capabilities unlock the product:

**LLM roleplay.** An LLM can play a credible buyer persona in a text or voice conversation, adapting to the rep's responses, pressing on weak handling, and scoring the interaction. This gives reps unlimited, low-stakes practice on the exact scenarios they will face in real calls.

**Continuous skill classification.** AI can infer skill scores from roleplay interactions, assessments, call analysis, and behavioral patterns — continuously, not just when a manager observes a call. This makes skill gaps visible in real time rather than at quarterly reviews.

**Coaching recommendation generation.** AI can generate specific, actionable coaching recommendations for managers based on skill data and deal context. Instead of "this rep needs help with objection handling," the system can generate "Alex's last three roleplays show consistent difficulty with pricing pushback — here are two specific exercises and the accounts where this is most likely to come up this week."

---

## The Coaching Queue as the Core Product Decision

The readiness score is the input signal. The coaching queue is where the product creates value. Most enablement analytics give managers data and leave them to figure out what to do with it. This product gives managers a specific, prioritised queue of coaching actions — who to coach, on what, and how.

That transition from "insight" to "recommended action" is the design decision that separates a reporting tool from an operational platform.

---

## The Broader Thesis

The B2B sales enablement market is large, saturated with legacy tools, and underserved by AI. The incumbents (Highspot, Seismic, Showpad) are content-storage platforms with analytics features. They are not readiness platforms. The opportunity is to define a new category — Revenue Readiness — that is continuous, AI-native, and outcome-connected, rather than event-based and content-centric.
