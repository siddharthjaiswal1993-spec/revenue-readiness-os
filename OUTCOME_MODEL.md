# Outcome Model — Revenue Readiness OS

---

## The Value Hypothesis

Revenue Readiness OS creates value by accelerating rep ramp time, improving coaching consistency, and connecting enablement activities to revenue outcomes. The core bet: if reps practice more (via AI roleplay), managers coach better (via prioritised coaching queues), and both activities are connected to pipeline and quota outcomes, revenue performance improves.

---

## Business Metrics

| Metric | Target | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Rep ramp time reduction | 30% faster to full productivity | The most direct enablement ROI metric |
| Quota attainment improvement | +10% on bottom quartile reps | Lifting the bottom quartile has more impact than improving the top |
| Coaching consistency score | >80% of reps coached on data-backed priorities | Consistency is the measurable behaviour the product drives |
| NRR | >120% | Skill gap analytics and deal readiness drive module expansion |

---

## Product Quality Metrics

| Metric | Target | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Readiness score calibration | Correlation >0.6 with 90-day quota attainment | The score only matters if it predicts real outcomes |
| Coaching recommendation acceptance | >55% | Managers who ignore recommendations are not getting value |
| Roleplay session completion rate | >80% of assigned sessions completed | Completion measures whether reps actually use the tool |
| Certification pass rate on first attempt | >70% | A low first-attempt pass rate indicates calibration issues |

---

## AI Quality Metrics

| Metric | Target | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Roleplay scoring inter-rater reliability | >80% agreement with human manager assessment | AI scoring must align with human judgment to build trust |
| Skill classification accuracy | >75% vs. manager assessment baseline | The readiness score depends on accurate skill classification |
| Content recommendation relevance | >70% rated relevant by rep | Irrelevant recommendations reduce engagement |

---

## The Metric That Matters Most

**Readiness score → quota attainment correlation.**

If the platform's composite readiness score predicts 90-day quota attainment at a meaningful correlation level, every downstream product decision (which skill dimensions to include, how to weight signals, when to surface coaching actions) becomes validated by revenue data. Without this correlation, the score is a satisfaction metric, not a revenue metric.

---

## Build / Maintain / Improve / Kill

**Build** — The outcome attribution model connecting readiness activities to pipeline and quota data. This is the metric that converts the platform from an enablement tool to a revenue intelligence platform.

**Maintain** — The roleplay engine and the readiness score. These are the core product surfaces that drive daily engagement.

**Improve** — The coaching recommendation specificity. "Alex needs objection handling coaching" is a good start; "here is the specific call where the gap was most visible, and here are two roleplay scenarios to use in this week's 1:1" is the product to get to.

**Kill** — Any feature that reduces review friction without maintaining coaching accountability. The coaching queue works because managers are accountable for acting on it. Remove that accountability and the product becomes a reporting dashboard.
