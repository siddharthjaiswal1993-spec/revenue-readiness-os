## What's Exemplary

**The AI roleplay design.** LLM-powered buyer conversation simulation is genuinely the right wedge for sales enablement. It is differentiable (Gong can analyse recorded calls; this creates practice opportunities before calls), measurable (scoring criteria are well-defined), and addresses the core rep development problem (you cannot get good at discovery without repeated, low-stakes practice).

**Readiness scoring as a composite.** Measuring rep readiness across 10 dimensions from multiple signal sources (roleplay, assessments, call data, behavioral patterns) and presenting it as an explainable composite score is the right product design. The key word is "explainable" — a score that managers cannot interpret is not actionable.

**The coaching queue design.** Giving frontline managers a prioritised action queue with specific coaching recommendations is solving the right manager problem. Managers know coaching matters; what they lack is structured guidance on who to coach on what. This is the gap the product fills.

**Explicit disclaimer on scope.** The README states clearly: "This is an independent conceptual portfolio project. It is not based on proprietary information from any company, does not represent the product roadmap of any organization, and does not contain any confidential or company-specific analysis." That clarity is the right standard for portfolio work.

## What Would Come Next

**LMS and CRM integration specs.** The product needs to connect to existing training infrastructure (Salesforce, Highspot, Seismic, Showpad, Gong). Specifying the integration model — which signals come from where, how they are normalised, and what the data freshness model looks like — would make the architecture credible.

**Roleplay scoring rubric.** The product defines that AI scores seller responses on "discovery, objection handling, value articulation, and close" but does not yet specify what a score of 4 vs. 3 means on each dimension. The scoring rubric is a PM design decision, not a model training detail.

**NRR model.** The business strategy would benefit from a clearer NRR model — which features drive retention vs. expansion, and what the typical expansion trigger looks like.
