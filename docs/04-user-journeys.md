## Journey 1: New AE Onboarding (30-Day Ramp)

**Trigger:** New Account Executive completes HR onboarding and is assigned to the platform.

**Actor:** New AE (e.g., Kavya Rao, joining Enterprise team) + Manager (Rohan Shah) + Enablement (Aanya Mehta)

**Current-State Pain:**
- Rep receives a shared onboarding checklist with 40+ items
- Content is organized by type, not by learning priority
- Manager does a weekly check-in but has no data on what the rep has actually learned
- No practice mechanism before the rep is in live customer situations
- Rep reaches 30 days with no reliable signal of readiness

**Future-State (AI-Native):**

*Day 1:*
- Platform generates a personalized 30-day ramp plan based on rep's prior experience, role, and team segment
- Plan includes daily learning milestones, skill targets, and roleplay checkpoints
- Manager receives ramp plan summary with weekly check-in prompts

*Days 1–10 (Foundation):*
- Adaptive modules on product, ICP, discovery methodology
- Daily AI-assessed micro-practices (5-min scenarios)
- Readiness score starts accumulating; manager sees progress daily

*Days 11–20 (Application):*
- Full roleplay scenarios: discovery call, product demo, objection handling
- AI scores each attempt; highlights specific improvement areas
- First certification checkpoint — must score ≥75 on discovery to advance
- Manager receives coaching brief: "Kavya is strong on product knowledge (88/100) but weak on discovery questioning (61/100). Recommended coaching focus: open-ended questioning and agenda-setting."

*Days 21–30 (Deal Readiness):*
- Deal-context roleplay: rep's first assigned accounts as scenarios
- Readiness score crosses certification threshold → rep cleared for first customer calls
- First 1:1 coaching session uses AI-generated prep brief with specific skill evidence

**Desired Outcome:**
- Rep reaches certified readiness in 30 days vs. 45-day average
- Manager has specific skill data for first coaching conversation
- Rep feels prepared and confident before first customer interaction

**Key Screens:** Ramp Progress Dashboard, Daily Roleplay Interface, Skill Score Tracker, Manager Coaching Brief

**Success Metrics:**
- Days to first certification milestone
- Skill score trajectory (30-day delta)
- Roleplay completion rate
- Rep confidence survey score
- Days to first qualified opportunity (compared to cohort baseline)

## Journey 2: Product Launch Enablement

**Trigger:** Product team announces new product feature/tier shipping in 6 weeks. Enablement must prepare the entire sales organization.

**Actor:** Aanya Mehta (VP Enablement) + Meera Iyer (PMM) + All AEs and SDRs

**Current-State Pain:**
- Enablement creates a training deck, schedules all-hands session, uploads to LMS
- Attendance is high at the live session; retention is low
- Reps can't find the updated battlecard when they need it
- 6 weeks later, messaging is inconsistent across teams
- No visibility into whether reps can actually handle competitive objections on the new product

**Future-State (AI-Native):**

*6 weeks before launch:*
- PMM uploads new messaging, positioning, and competitive response to platform
- AI generates: content summary, key messaging pillars, objection prediction map
- Launch readiness program auto-generated with adaptive paths per role (AE, SDR, SE)

*4 weeks before launch:*
- Reps complete foundation modules (product knowledge, positioning)
- AI roleplay scenarios: "Buyer is evaluating us vs. incumbent on new product capability"
- Certification checkpoint: must achieve 80/100 on product knowledge before advancing

*2 weeks before launch:*
- Manager coaching queue shows reps below threshold on competitive messaging
- Managers receive: "3 reps on your team need competitive objection coaching before launch. Here are their scores and a 30-min coaching script."
- AI generates deal-specific content bundles for reps with active competitive evaluations

*Launch week:*
- Readiness dashboard: "87% of AEs certified. 4 reps below threshold — here is their targeted coaching plan."
- Post-launch: messaging consistency scoring from call intelligence feeds back into program quality score

**Desired Outcome:**
- Launch day: ≥85% of reps certified on new product messaging
- Post-launch: competitive win rate on new product maintained or improved
- PMM has data showing field messaging adoption and consistency

**Key Screens:** Program Builder, Launch Readiness Dashboard, Manager Coaching Queue, Content Performance Analytics

## Journey 3: Manager Coaching Cycle (Weekly)

**Trigger:** Monday morning — manager opens platform to prepare for the week's coaching.

**Actor:** Rohan Shah (Frontline Manager)

**Current-State Pain:**
- Manager opens CRM, reviews pipeline, identifies deals at risk
- Coaching is deal-centric, not skill-centric
- Every rep gets a similar conversation ("what's the status?")
- No data on what each rep's specific skill gaps are
- Coaching time is consumed by deal review rather than skill development

**Future-State (AI-Native):**

*Monday, 8:00 AM:*
- Platform surfaces weekly coaching brief: "This week's highest-priority reps based on readiness score + pipeline risk"
- Coaching queue shows: Arjun Nair (SDR) — discovery score dropped 15 points; Kavya Rao (AE) — competitive positioning weak on 3 active deals

*For each rep in queue:*
- AI generates coaching brief: specific skill gap, evidence (roleplay scores, call patterns), suggested coaching approach, recommended roleplay to assign
- One-click: schedule 1:1 with pre-loaded coaching agenda
- Post-coaching: manager logs outcome; platform tracks skill trajectory

*Friday review:*
- Platform sends weekly summary: "3 coaching actions taken this week. Kavya's competitive positioning score improved 8 points. Arjun's discovery score unchanged — escalate to enablement."

**Desired Outcome:**
- Manager coaches 100% of at-risk reps each week with specific skill-based talking points
- Rep skill scores trend upward in coached areas
- Manager time-to-coaching insight: < 5 minutes per rep

**Success Metrics:**
- Manager coaching action completion rate (target: ≥80%)
- Skill score improvement for coached reps (vs. uncoached control)
- Manager time saved in 1:1 preparation

## Journey 4: Rep AI Roleplay Practice

**Trigger:** Kavya Rao (AE) has a high-stakes competitive evaluation call in 3 days. She wants to practice.

**Actor:** Kavya Rao (AE)

**Current-State Pain:**
- Kavya asks a colleague to roleplay — schedule doesn't align
- She reviews the competitive battlecard on her own
- Goes into the call underprepared for the specific objections she will face

**Future-State (AI-Native):**

*Step 1 — Scenario Selection:*
- Platform surfaces recommended scenario: "Competitive evaluation with VP Operations at a manufacturing company. Buyer has been using a legacy system for 5 years and is concerned about implementation risk."
- Kavya can customize: buyer persona, deal stage, primary objection focus

*Step 2 — Roleplay Session:*
- AI plays the buyer: asks probing questions, raises objections, responds to Kavya's prompts realistically
- Live scoring panel shows real-time performance indicators
- Session length: 15–20 minutes

*Step 3 — Debrief:*
- AI generates structured feedback: what worked, what didn't, specific improvement suggestions
- Scores updated across relevant skills: competitive positioning (+4), objection handling (-2 on pricing)
- Suggested follow-up: "Review the implementation risk objection handling module before your call."

*Step 4 — Manager Visibility:*
- Manager receives notification: "Kavya completed a competitive evaluation roleplay. Score: 72/100. See debrief."

**Desired Outcome:**
- Kavya enters the real call more prepared than she would be without practice
- Manager has signal on rep readiness before a high-stakes call
- Each roleplay improves the platform's model of Kavya's skill profile
