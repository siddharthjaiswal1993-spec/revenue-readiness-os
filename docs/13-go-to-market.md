# Go-to-Market Strategy

---

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

**Primary ICP:**
- B2B SaaS or technology company
- 100–2,000 quota-carrying reps
- Dedicated sales enablement function (≥1 FTE)
- Active growth (≥20% YoY headcount growth OR recent product expansion)
- Formal onboarding process exists but performance is inconsistent
- Management team can articulate a ramp time problem or win rate inconsistency problem

**Qualifying signals:**
- Recent investment in CRM and call intelligence tools (signals data maturity)
- Active hiring in sales and enablement roles
- VP Enablement or equivalent role exists and reports to CRO
- Previous LMS or training tool in use (demonstrates category awareness)
- Company at Series B or later (budget availability and organizational maturity)

**Disqualifying signals:**
- Fewer than 25 quota-carrying reps (not enough scale for ROI)
- No dedicated enablement function (no internal champion for adoption)
- Sales team primarily transactional (short cycle, high volume — readiness less differentiated)
- No CRM in use (lack of deal data makes revenue correlation impossible)

---

## Buyer and User Personas

**Economic buyer:** CRO or VP Sales — cares about ramp time, win rate, quota attainment
**Champion:** VP Sales Enablement — cares about program effectiveness, skill improvement, content adoption
**Influencer:** Frontline Sales Manager — cares about coaching queue quality, rep risk visibility
**End user:** AE, SDR — cares about personal development, deal prep, ease of use
**Technical evaluator:** RevOps or IT — cares about integrations, security, data governance

**Typical deal dynamics:**
- Champion (VP Enablement) drives evaluation
- CRO signs off on business case
- RevOps/IT reviews security and integration requirements
- Manager resistance is the most common late-stage objection ("my team doesn't have time for more tools")

## Positioning

**Category:** AI-native revenue readiness platform

**Positioning statement:**
"Revenue Readiness OS is the AI-native platform for B2B revenue organizations that want to accelerate rep ramp, build consistent coaching discipline, and connect enablement investment to revenue outcomes — replacing fragmented training systems and intuition-based coaching with a continuous, measurable, AI-powered readiness operating system."

**Key differentiation points:**
1. **Continuous readiness measurement** — not a training completion dashboard; a live, multi-signal skill intelligence system
2. **AI roleplay at scale** — unlimited, consistent, scored practice without scheduling human roleplays
3. **Manager-first coaching** — AI-prioritized coaching queue with specific, evidence-based recommendations, not generic reports
4. **Revenue connection** — readiness-to-win-rate and readiness-to-ramp-time correlation as a native platform feature, not a custom analytics project
5. **Governance built in** — explainable AI outputs, human override, bias auditing — designed for enterprise adoption

## Packaging and Pricing Hypothesis

**Tiers:**

| Tier | Target | Included |
|------|--------|---------|
| **Readiness** | SMB / early enablement | Readiness scores, content hub, basic certifications, manager dashboard |
| **Enablement** | Growth / mid-market | + AI Roleplay, Coaching Queue, personalized learning paths, call intelligence integration |
| **Revenue** | Enterprise | + Deal Readiness, CRM write integration, executive insights, win-rate correlation, advanced analytics |

**Pricing model:** Per seat per year (rep + manager seats separately priced; manager seats at 2× rep seat price due to higher value density)

**Indicative pricing:**
- Readiness tier: $30–50/rep/month
- Enablement tier: $60–90/rep/month
- Revenue tier: $100–150/rep/month + platform fee at large deployments

**Expansion motion:**
- Expand from one segment (Enterprise AEs) to adjacent segments (SDRs, SEs, Customer Success)
- Expand from one use case (onboarding) to adjacent use cases (product launch enablement, competitive enablement, manager development)
- Expand from one geography to multi-region deployments

## Sales Motion

**Velocity:** Land-expand; typical initial deal 100–300 seats; expand to 500–1,500+ seats within 18 months

**Sales cycle:** 45–90 days for initial deployment; driven by champion + CRO alignment

**POC/trial:** 30-day pilot with one team segment (e.g., new hire cohort or one manager's team); clear success criteria defined upfront

**Proof of value:** 
- Primary: Time-to-first-readiness-score (demonstrated in first week)
- Secondary: Manager coaching action completion rate improvement at 30 days
- Tertiary: Rep readiness score improvement at 60–90 days

**Common objections:**
- "We already have an LMS" → "This is the intelligence layer above your LMS. It connects your existing content and programs to skill data and revenue outcomes."
- "Reps won't use another tool" → "The rep experience is designed to be personal and valuable. The platform gives reps a reason to return — deal prep, personal development, practice on demand."
- "We can't prove ROI" → "We show you the correlation data within 90 days of deployment. We define the success metrics upfront."
- "How does AI know our business?" → "The platform adapts to your skill definitions, your buyer personas, your deal data. AI learns your context, not a generic template."

## ROI Narrative

**Ramp time reduction:**
If a company hires 50 AEs per year with a 9-month average ramp time, and the platform reduces ramp time by 2 months (22%):
- Value = 50 AEs × 2 months × (Average monthly AE quota contribution)
- At $100K/month average quota rate: **$10M in recovered pipeline capacity per year**

**Coaching efficiency:**
If managers currently spend 3 hours/week on coaching prep and the platform reduces this to 30 minutes:
- Value = 20 managers × 2.5 hours/week × 48 weeks × ($150K/year manager cost ÷ 2,000 hours)
- **$180K per year in manager time recovered**

**Win rate improvement:**
If companies with ≥70 average readiness score maintain a 4-point higher win rate on competitive deals:
- At 100 competitive deals/year at $80K ACV average, 4-point improvement = 4 additional wins
- **$320K in incremental revenue per year** attributable to readiness improvement
