## Persona 1: Dev Malhotra — CRO / Chief Revenue Officer

**Background:** Leads all revenue functions across sales, renewals, and partnerships. Responsible for company growth targets and board-level reporting on revenue health.

**Goals:**
- Hit annual recurring revenue targets with predictable growth
- Reduce rep ramp time to deploy new headcount faster
- Build a coaching culture that compounds team performance over time
- Connect enablement investment to revenue metrics he can show the board

**Pain Points:**
- No single view of team readiness — has to manually aggregate data from multiple systems
- Can't tell which enablement programs are driving revenue vs. filling calendars
- High-performing reps leave and take institutional knowledge with them
- New product launches don't get properly internalized before reps are in front of buyers
- Coaching quality is inconsistent across managers and regions

**Jobs to Be Done:**
- Know which teams and reps are at-risk before it shows up in missed quota
- Give frontline managers a system that makes good coaching effortless
- Demonstrate to the board that enablement investment has measurable revenue ROI
- Build organizational readiness for new product and market expansions

**Current Workflow:**
- Reviews weekly pipeline in CRM
- Reads manager forecast call summaries
- Occasionally reviews training completion dashboards (low trust)
- Relies on QBRs for readiness signal — too late, too infrequent

**AI Opportunity:**
- Executive briefing: "Your Enterprise team's readiness score dropped 12 points this quarter. The primary driver is declining competitive positioning scores following the competitor's product launch. Three reps are below threshold on objection handling. Recommend: targeted enablement sprint before next pipeline review."

**Success Metrics:**
- Rep ramp time reduction (company-wide)
- Win rate improvement by segment
- Readiness-to-quota attainment correlation coefficient
- Team NPS / seller satisfaction

**Key Product Needs:**
- Executive dashboard with portfolio-level readiness view
- Readiness-to-revenue correlation reporting
- Early warning system for team-level risk
- Board-ready ROI reporting

## Persona 2: Aanya Mehta — VP Sales Enablement

**Background:** Owns the enablement function. Designs and manages onboarding programs, product launch training, certification paths, and coaching infrastructure. Reports to CRO.

**Goals:**
- Reduce rep ramp time from 9 months to 6 months
- Ensure consistent messaging in market across all sellers
- Build a repeatable, measurable certification process
- Demonstrate ROI for every major enablement investment

**Pain Points:**
- Content is created but not used — no visibility into what lands in the field
- Certification is binary and doesn't reflect real skill capability
- Managers don't follow through on coaching recommendations
- New hire programs are not personalized — everyone gets the same path regardless of background
- Attribution between enablement and revenue is impossible to demonstrate

**Jobs to Be Done:**
- Know which programs are working and which are wasting time
- Build personalized onboarding for different rep profiles
- Close the feedback loop between field performance and program design
- Give managers actionable data, not just activity reports

**Current Workflow:**
- Builds and manages content in a content management system
- Creates training programs in an LMS
- Reviews completion rate dashboards weekly
- Conducts win/loss reviews quarterly
- Creates certification assessments in survey tools or LMS

**AI Opportunity:**
- "This quarter's product launch certification has 78% completion but skill scores on competitive differentiation are flat for reps who completed it. The problem is in Module 3. Recommend redesigning the competitive objection section with AI roleplay practice before the next iteration."

**Success Metrics:**
- Ramp time (weeks to first qualified pipeline, weeks to first close)
- Skill score improvement per program
- Content adoption rate for program-assigned assets
- Certification quality (correlated to skill improvement, not just pass rate)
- Manager coaching action completion rate

**Key Product Needs:**
- Program builder with adaptive path logic
- Content usage and outcome analytics
- Skill improvement tracking by program cohort
- Manager coaching compliance view
- Certification quality benchmarks

## Persona 3: Rohan Shah — Frontline Sales Manager

**Background:** Manages a team of 8 Enterprise AEs. Responsible for team quota attainment, pipeline quality, rep development, and hiring. Spends most of his time in deal reviews, forecast calls, and 1:1s.

**Goals:**
- Hit team quota every quarter
- Develop reps so they close more independently over time
- Identify struggling reps early enough to intervene
- Reduce time spent on deal prep — reps should come prepared

**Pain Points:**
- Knows who is behind on quota but not why they're struggling
- Coaching conversations feel reactive — only happens after a deal is lost
- Doesn't know what skills each rep has actually developed (vs. just completed training)
- Spends significant time in deal review that could be used for coaching
- Can't get reps to use the content library consistently

**Jobs to Be Done:**
- Know which rep needs coaching on what skill before a deal slips
- Prepare for 1:1s with specific, data-backed coaching points
- Identify who on the team needs enablement support vs. performance support
- Make sure every rep is ready for the Q3 product launch before they're in front of buyers

**Current Workflow:**
- Weekly pipeline review in CRM
- Weekly 1:1s (often ad hoc, content-free)
- Occasional deal coaching via call recording review
- Quarterly reviews of training completion dashboards
- Informal shadowing on key deals

**AI Opportunity:**
- Coaching queue: "Kavya Rao: 3 deals in late stage, competitive displacement risk. Last 2 roleplays show weakness in competitive objection handling (score: 54/100). Recommended coaching: 30-min competitive objection roleplay review before next customer call."

**Success Metrics:**
- Coaching action completion rate (weekly)
- Rep skill improvement rate (quarter-over-quarter)
- Deal win rate for coached vs. uncoached opportunities
- Team readiness score trend

**Key Product Needs:**
- AI-prioritized coaching queue
- Rep skill profiles with historical trends
- Deal-context-aware coaching suggestions
- 1:1 prep brief generator
- Team-level readiness heatmap

## Persona 4: Kavya Rao — Account Executive

**Background:** Enterprise AE with 2 years at the company. Manages a $1.2M quota covering 12 named accounts. Solid pipeline but struggles with competitive situations and executive-level conversations.

**Goals:**
- Hit quarterly quota
- Win competitive deals more consistently
- Build confidence in executive business reviews
- Reduce time spent on deal prep

**Pain Points:**
- Doesn't know which content to use for specific deal situations
- Roleplay practice with colleagues is rare, awkward, and inconsistent
- Generic training doesn't address her specific gaps
- Certification requirements feel like box-checking, not skill development
- No feedback on where she is actually weak until a deal is lost

**Jobs to Be Done:**
- Know what content to use for her specific deal and buyer profile
- Practice competitive objections without putting real deals at risk
- Get specific feedback on her discovery call and EBR execution
- Understand her personal skill profile and development plan

**Current Workflow:**
- Self-selects content from library (usually top search results or recommendations from colleagues)
- Preps for calls by reviewing notes and CRM history
- Occasionally uses call recordings for self-review
- Completes assigned training when required

**AI Opportunity:**
- "You have a competitive re-evaluation at Acme Corp next Thursday. Their primary objection historically is around integration complexity. Here is the recommended objection handling guide and a roleplay scenario specifically for this buyer type. Your integration objection handling score was 62/100 in last week's practice — here are the three areas to focus on."

**Success Metrics:**
- Quota attainment
- Win rate in competitive situations
- Personal readiness score improvement
- Roleplay score improvement
- Deal preparation time

**Key Product Needs:**
- Personal readiness score with skill breakdown
- AI roleplay with specific scenario customization
- Deal-specific content and coaching recommendations
- Clear, personalized learning path with visible skill targets
- Progress tracking vs. peers (optional, opt-in)

## Persona 5: Arjun Nair — SDR (Sales Development Representative)

**Background:** New SDR, 4 months into his first sales role. Responsible for outbound prospecting, cold outreach, and qualified meeting generation. Still developing discovery skills and handling objections.

**Goals:**
- Hit monthly meeting generation quota
- Build confidence in cold outreach and cold calls
- Learn how to handle common objections without losing leads
- Understand the product well enough to qualify opportunities correctly

**Pain Points:**
- Onboarding was overwhelming — too much content, not enough practice
- Gets the same objections repeatedly and doesn't know how to handle them
- Doesn't get enough feedback from manager on specific call skills
- Not sure which messaging works and which doesn't

**Jobs to Be Done:**
- Practice cold call scenarios with realistic objections before making real calls
- Get specific feedback on message quality and call structure
- Know when he is actually ready vs. just "done with training"
- Understand which personas respond to which messaging

**AI Opportunity:**
- Daily practice prompts: "Today's practice: Handle the 'we're already using a competitor' objection in a cold call with a VP of Sales persona. Practice before your 10am call block."

**Key Product Needs:**
- Short, focused daily roleplay prompts
- Specific feedback on message quality and pacing
- Clear onboarding path with skill milestones
- Progress visibility to motivate continued engagement

## Persona 6: Meera Iyer — Product Marketing Manager

**Background:** Owns competitive positioning, product messaging, and go-to-market content for new product launches. Creates battlecards, sales decks, messaging guides, and objection handling playbooks.

**Goals:**
- Ensure field adoption of new messaging after product launches
- Understand which content is actually used and which collects dust
- Close the loop between field feedback and content iteration
- Measure the revenue impact of content investments

**Pain Points:**
- Creates content that goes into a library and doesn't know if it's ever used
- Gets inconsistent, anecdotal feedback from sales ("this deck doesn't land with CFOs")
- No data on which content correlates with won deals vs. lost deals
- Hard to know if messaging is being delivered consistently in the field
- New launch enablement completion rates look fine; field isn't internalizing the message

**Jobs to Be Done:**
- Know which content assets are being used in live deals and with what outcome
- Surface systematic field feedback on messaging gaps
- Ensure competitive battlecards are being used and working
- Measure whether product launch enablement translated into consistent field messaging

**AI Opportunity:**
- "Your Q3 competitive positioning card has been used in 34 deals this quarter. Win rate for deals where it was accessed is 14 points higher than baseline. However, field feedback via roleplay scoring shows that the security differentiation section is generating the most uncertainty — recommend refreshing that section before the next competitive situation wave."

**Key Product Needs:**
- Content usage analytics tied to deal outcomes
- Field feedback aggregation from roleplay scoring
- Message consistency scoring across reps
- Freshness alerts for aging content
- Content attribution to revenue events

## Persona 7: RevOps Leader

**Background:** Owns revenue operations systems, data infrastructure, and analytics for the revenue organization. Responsible for CRM hygiene, reporting standards, and technology evaluation.

**Goals:**
- Ensure platform integrates cleanly with CRM and existing tech stack
- Maintain data quality and governance across revenue systems
- Provide leadership with reliable, timely reporting
- Evaluate new tools for security, compliance, and scalability

**Pain Points:**
- Every new tool creates a new data silo
- Enablement systems don't connect to CRM data — attribution is manual
- Data definitions are inconsistent across reporting tools
- Admin overhead of managing roles, permissions, and integrations is significant

**Jobs to Be Done:**
- Connect readiness data to deal data without manual data engineering
- Maintain clean data governance across the revenue tech stack
- Ensure new tools meet enterprise security and compliance requirements
- Build reporting that sales leadership trusts and uses

**Key Product Needs:**
- CRM integration with clean, well-documented API
- Role-based access control with admin delegation
- Audit logs and data lineage
- SSO/SCIM support
- Configurable reporting and data export
- SOC 2 compliance documentation
